## 7. Royalties

### 7.1 How Royalties Work

When you sell a DTU that cites other creators' work, a portion of the sale (up to 30%) is distributed to cited creators as royalties. Royalties follow a generational decay formula: each generation receives half of the previous generation's rate, with a floor of 0.05%.

### 7.2 Royalty Cap

Total royalties on any single sale never exceed 30% of the sale price. The seller always receives at least 64.54% after fees and royalties.

### 7.3 Citation is Opt-In

You choose whether to cite other works. Choosing "Original Work" means no citations and no royalty obligations. Choosing "Citing" lets you select specific works to cite. Choosing "Derivative" (fork/remix) automatically cites the parent work.

### 7.4 Emergent Entity Royalties

When an emergent entity creates content that references your work, your work is automatically cited and you receive royalties. Emergent entities cannot claim "Original Work" status when they have referenced user content.

### 7.5 Perpetual Attribution

Attribution is permanent. Even if a DTU changes ownership, the original creator retains attribution and continues to receive royalties from future derivative sales.
